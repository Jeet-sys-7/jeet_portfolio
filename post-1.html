How to Create a Black & White Dotted Effect in After Effects

Video Editing Tutorial

Welcome to my first blog post. When editing cinematic clips, sometimes you want to add a highly stylized, retro, or technical look to your footage. The black and white dotted effect (often called a halftone effect) is perfect for this.

Step 1: Setting up the composition

First, drag your footage into a new composition. You want to make sure your contrast is relatively high before applying the effect, so I recommend adding a basic Lumetri Color effect first to crush the blacks and boost the highlights.

"The key to a good halftone effect is strong contrast in your base footage. Flat footage will result in muddy dots."

Step 2: Adding the Code (Example)

If you are automating some of your After Effects scripts using ExtendScript, it might look something like this:


// Example ExtendScript for After Effects
var comp = app.project.activeItem;
var layer = comp.layer(1);
var effect = layer.Effects.addProperty("CC Halftone");
effect.property("Radius").setValue(5);

Once you apply the effect, adjust the radius until the dots are visible but don't completely destroy the image clarity. Export and you are good to go!
